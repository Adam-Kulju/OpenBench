Ethereal 13.50

Ethereal 13.50 (NNUE) has been released. In addition to the usual updates to the search and evaluation algorithms, Ethereal is now using a newly trained NNUE architecture called "Mirrored Half-KP". Ethereal is one of the first engines to adopt this improvement which drastically reduces training times, Network size, and improves symetrical evaluations over the board.

Ethereal 13.50 (NNUE) includes two NNUE Networks for evaluation. These Networks continue Ethereal's long standing legacy of original work. These networks are not trained on, nor duplicated from the works of any other Computer Chess team. With it, Ethereal continues to refine new ideas and techniques, allowing older processors to outperform NNUE implementations found in other engines, while still harnessing the full power of the AVX/AVX2 instruction sets for CPUs with support.

Additionally, Ethereal is still the first engine to offer a secondary Network trained exclusively for Fischer Random Chess (FRC). This Network outperforms the standard Network by significant margins in FRC play, showcasing a creative style by Ethereal, not reliant on Opening Theory. These Neural Networks offer up to +125 elo over Ethereal 13.25 according to the CCRL rating list for Fischer Random Chess, the culmination of over over a hundred million games played between various versions of Ethereal.

The commercial NNUE version requires a CPU with the SSSE3 instruction set (which almost all CPUs have), and will not run on systems lacking these instructions. AVX/AVX2 have become ubiquitous in modern processor design, and are necessary to capitalize on the full strength of Neural Network technology.
